Ginger Ale Salad (Jello), Interpreted

Ingredients

3 Packets Knox Gelatine

20 oz can Crushed Pineapple

29 oz can Sliced Pears

2 tbsp Sugar

2 tbsp Lemon Juice

16 oz Ginger Ale (or non-alcoholic ginger beer)

4 oz Water

Vegetable oil or Pam spray

Directions

NOTES:
1. Water can be omitted for a sturdier jello. Just use juice to dissolve gelatine instead.

2. Recipe assumes 1 cup liquid per packet of gelatine.

3. The “fizz” of this dish comes more from the ginger flavor of the ginger ale/beer than from the carbonation, but both are nice. Punchy ginger flavor is recommended.

4. If not using a mould, ginger ale salad can be made and set in mixing bowl instead (less pouring, less carbonation loss).

PREPARE:
1. Clean and coat with vegetable oil 1-2 moulds or containers (approximately 75oz volume needed for recipe)

2. Have a large pan of cold tap water into which a large mixing bowl can sit without spilling.

3. Strain cans of fruit to collect the liquid. Stir together. Keep 1 cup.

4. Chop pear slices into smaller (bite-sized) pieces, as needed.

5. Measure 2 tablespoons of lemon juice.

6. Make space in the fridge for your moulds.

COOK:
1. Heat half of crushed pineapple plus 1/2 cup of juice in a pan until bubbly.

2. In the large mixing bowl, stir all 3 packets of gelatine into 4 oz water (about 1 minute).

4. Once pineapple is heated and bubbly, add it to the gelatine mixture. Add in pears, remaining juice, lemon juice, and sugar. Stir about 30 seconds until combined.

5. Move mixing bowl into pan of cold tap water, continue stirring to reduce heat of mixture.

6. Carefully measure the 16 oz ginger ale/beer, trying to keep the carbonation as much as possible.

7. Carefully pour measured ginger ale/beer into mixture, and gently stir until just combined, trying to keep the carbonation.

8. Pour mixture into mould(s).

9. Refrigerate 3 hours or until firm.
