Pork Dumplings

Dinner

Ingredients

Filling

1 lb ground pork (80/20 or 70/30 fat content works best)

1 heaping tablespoon grated or finely chopped ginger

1 cup chopped chives or green onions

1 cup chopped mushrooms (shiitakes or button crimini)

2+ teaspoons sesame oil

1 tablespoon soy sauce

1 egg

½ teaspoon salt (plus more for boiling water)

1 ½ teaspoon sugar


Wrappers

1 package of round dumpling wrappers, we used Myojo brand gyoza/pot sticker wrappers


These can be found in Asian & Specialty Grocery stores (Uwajimaya, Asian Family Market, H-Mart, Ballard Market) and are typically located in the refrigerated noodle section. Preferred brand is Twin Marquis, but any round wheat dumpling skin will work.

Northern Style - thick dumpling skin (preferred for boiled dumplings)

Shanghai Style - thin dumpling skin (preferred for steamed dumplings)


Sauce

2 teaspoons sugar (we forgot this the first time and it was good)

1 tablespoon rice vinegar or brown rice vinegar

2 tablespoons soy sauce


Optional:

Laoganma to taste

Ginger to taste

Scallions to taste

Description

Recipe makes enough for 52 dumps

Directions

1. Prepare a baking sheet or large plate with wax paper or parchment paper

2. Prepare a large bowl for the filling

3. Fill a large pot of water and place it on the stove. Do not turn on the burner yet. (You’ll boil 6 dumplings at a time.)

4. Prepare a small bowl of water and a clean fork for each person participating.

5. Combine all the filling ingredients in a bowl and mix thoroughly with your hands or a spatula.

6. Start the water to boil and add salt.

7. Take a wrapper in one hand and use a fork to drop a rounded tablespoon of filling into the center.

8. Dip your finger in the water and wet the circumference of the dump.

9. Fold the dump into a taco shape with the top open, then pinch together one end.

10. Use one finger inside the taco to create folds while the other hand’s index finger and thumb pinch the fold and the sides together. (I found it easiest to fold on just one side.) Work your way along the length of the taco until the opening is completely sealed.

To freeze, put tray of uncooked dumps in freezer for 2 hours, then transfer to a ziplock bag.

Boiling Your Dumps
11. Place 6 dumps in the boiling water for 6 minutes.

12. While the dumps are cooking, combine the sauce ingredients (or let someone else do this while you fold the dumps. Whatever.)

13. Strain out the dumps once cooked, and put them on a plate.

14. Dunk the dumps in the delicious sauce and put them in your face. Serve with cooked green things.

To Pan-Fry Instead of Boiling
1. Heat oil in a steel pan over medium-high heat until oil shimmers.

2. Add as many dumps as you desire so long as they don’t touch.

3. Cook for 15-20 SECONDS

4. Add ½ cup water and cover the pan

5. Let cook for 6 minutes

6. Uncover and cook until water has boiled off

7. Flip dumps and cook until desired crispiness on other side.

8. Dip in delicious sauce

9. Add to face-hole

Notes

Try using 1.5-2 cups of green onions (2 bunches). Still ok with 85/15 meat. Serve with green veggie like green beans, and 9 dumps + greebies is a good serving size.

Putting a damp napkin over the wrappers on the counter helps keep them from drying.
